## The 5-minute story pitch (~720 words, rubric-ordered)

Timing markers in brackets. Live demo where marked. Open and close on the same line — judges remember the bookend.

> **[0:00 — Hook, 20s]**
> "Picture your dream job interview. You've got the skills. But the moment they ask 'tell me about yourself,' your mind goes blank and your voice shakes. You don't lose because you weren't good enough — you lose because nobody ever taught you how to *speak*."

> **[0:20 — Problem + SDG, 45s]**
> "Here's the gap. Speaking decides interviews, promotions, pitches — and yet it's almost never taught in school. The only fix today is private coaching, which runs upwards of RM200 a session. So the people who most need help — students, first jobseekers, anyone without money to spend — get locked out. This is a direct barrier to **UN SDG 4: Quality Education** — the skills that decide opportunity aren't accessible to everyone."

> **[1:05 — Solution, 40s]**
> "So we built SpeakBold: a free, AI-powered speaking coach that runs entirely in your browser. You speak, and within seconds our AI scores your rhetoric, clarity, and pacing — and tells you exactly what to fix, the way a RM200 coach would. No cost. No geography. No judgment."

> **[1:45 — How it works / LIVE DEMO, 90s]**
> "Let me show you. *(Demo)* When you sign up, you pick a goal and you're speaking within a minute — a placement test hears you and meets you at your level, so you're never bored or overwhelmed. From there you get a personalised **Pathway** that builds every part of a strong speaker, tier by tier. When you want to practise for real, there's **The Lab** — interview, pitch, impromptu, and storytelling drills — and here's the part nobody else does: *(turn on camera)* SpeakBold reads your **body language** live — posture, expression, gestures — all processed on your own device, nothing uploaded, not even to us. And when you're ready to compete, **The Arena**: live one-on-one debates against AI personas or a real human opponent on another phone, judged by AI, climbing a chess-style ELO ladder."

> **[3:15 — Impact, 40s]**
> "And this is already real. Eighty-one learners found us with zero marketing. They've completed five hundred drills — two hundred and twenty-nine minutes of analyzed speech — and three out of four of them entered the curriculum. A skill that used to cost RM200 a session is now free for anyone with a browser, 24/7, with zero fear of judgment. That's SDG 4 in practice — not awareness, *access*."

> **[3:55 — Risks & Mitigation, 35s]**
> "We've thought hard about the risks. AI can mis-grade — so every score is anchored to a fixed rubric, clamped against hallucination, and always paired with human-readable feedback. People try to cheat — real users have spoken prompt-injections into the mic and even attacked our database — so judging runs server-side where the client can't touch it, and every input is sanitized in depth. Privacy matters — so the camera analysis never leaves your device. And to keep it free at scale, we run a fallback chain across four AI providers so it stays cheap and reliable."

> **[4:30 — Next steps + Team + Close, 30s]**
> "Next, we're partnering with schools, adding teacher dashboards and more languages, and shipping tournament mode for inter-school debate. We're Group Qwerty — *(one line: who built what)*. We started SpeakBold because speaking shouldn't be a privilege you pay for. Try it yourself — speakbold.vercel.app. Because the room — and the world — leans in when you speak. Thank you."

## Delivery notes

- **The live body-language demo is your winning moment** — rehearse it until it can't fail; keep a recorded fallback clip in case the venue camera/mic misbehaves.
- ~720 words ≈ 4:45–5:00 at a confident pace. Running long? Cut the placement-test sentence in the demo beat first.
- Numbers to memorize: **81 / 504 / 229 / 74%**. Say them as words in the pitch ("eighty-one learners") — spoken digits sound like a spreadsheet.
- When you say "nothing uploaded, not even to us," **pause**. Privacy lands hard with judges.
- The risks beat is a scoring section most teams skip — deliver it with pride, not apology. The "users attacked us and the defense held" line signals maturity.
- If a judge looks skeptical at "AI judge," invite them: *"Try to flatter it or trick it — we sanitize for prompt injection."* Confidence sells.

## Phrases to avoid

- "It's basically like ChatGPT for..." (undersells the system)
- "We hope to..." → say "Next, we ship..."
- "Only 81 users" → say "81 organic users with zero marketing spend"

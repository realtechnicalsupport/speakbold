# SpeakBold — Why It's Worth It

*Plain-language Q&A about the value of the app — not the tech stack. Use this for "why should anyone care" conversations: with judges, with users, with anyone asking "okay but why does this matter."*

---

### "What is SpeakBold, in one sentence?"
> It's a free app that helps you get better at speaking — interviews, presentations, debates, pitches — by having you actually speak, and telling you what to fix, every time.

### "Who is this actually for?"
> Anyone who's ever frozen up before a big conversation: a student before an interview, someone prepping for a presentation, a person who just wants to sound more confident. You don't need to be "bad" at speaking to get something out of it — most people have never had anyone watch them speak and tell them, specifically, what to improve.

### "Speaking coaches already exist. Why does this need to be an app?"
> Because a real coach costs around RM150 a session — that's a price tag most students and jobseekers can't justify, even though speaking well affects almost every interview, pitch, and presentation in their life. SpeakBold gives the same kind of specific, useful feedback for free, whenever you want it, as many times as you want.

### "Okay, but what's the catch? Nothing free is *actually* free."
> The catch is honesty about where we are: it's free because the AI feedback costs us almost nothing to run, and we built it because we think this should exist, not because we've figured out how to extract money from you later. If we ever charge anyone, it'll be schools and institutions — not the person practicing.

### "I'm a nervous/bad speaker. Will this actually help, or just make me feel worse?"
> This is the opposite of a pop quiz. The feedback is built to be specific and encouraging — it tells you what worked and what to try next, not just a score. And nobody else hears it. You can mess up a hundred times with zero audience, which is exactly the practice you can't get in real life.

### "How is this different from just talking to myself, or to ChatGPT?"
> Talking to yourself gives you no feedback. Talking to ChatGPT gives you feedback on what you *typed* — SpeakBold listens to how you actually *said* it: your pacing, your structure, even your body language if you turn the camera on. It's built specifically around the skill of speaking, not just "having a conversation with an AI."

### "Will the AI just tell me what I want to hear?"
> No — it scores against a fixed rubric (structure, clarity, pacing, persuasion), so the feedback is grounded in something real, not vibes. It's encouraging in *tone*, but it will tell you what's actually weak. The goal is feedback you'd trust, not feedback that flatters you.

### "What if I want to actually compete, not just practice?"
> That's the Arena — live debates against an AI opponent or another person, with a ranking system (like a video game ladder) so you can see yourself climb. For people who are motivated by competition rather than just self-improvement, this turns practice into a game.

### "Can I actually tell if I'm improving, or does it just feel like busywork?"
> Your very first recording becomes your baseline forever, and the Growth Report shows your progress against it over time — real numbers, your own voice, then and now. You don't have to take our word for it that you're getting better; you can see it.

### "Is my camera/voice being watched by some company somewhere?"
> Your voice recordings are private to you and deletable. Your camera is the bigger promise: body-language feedback (posture, gestures, expression) is processed entirely on your own device — the video never gets uploaded anywhere, not even to us. If you're camera-shy, there's also a way to see how it works without turning your camera on at all.

### "How much time does this actually take?"
> A single drill can be as short as 30 seconds. You don't need to block out an hour — open the app, speak for half a minute, get feedback, done. The structured curriculum (Pathway) is there if you want a longer journey, but the door-in is tiny.

### "Is this just a school project, or could this be something real?"
> The product is live right now — real people found it with zero marketing and have already completed hundreds of practice sessions. The bigger vision is schools using it to teach a skill that's almost never formally taught, in places where hiring a speaking coach was never an option. That's not a "someday" — the free, in-browser version that makes that possible already exists today.

### "What's the one thing you want someone to walk away believing?"
> That the ability to speak well — clearly, confidently, persuasively — shouldn't depend on whether your family could afford to pay for it. We built the cheapest possible way to get genuinely useful feedback on your speaking, and made it free, because that gap shouldn't exist.
